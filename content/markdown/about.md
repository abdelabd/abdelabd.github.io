I'm broadly interested in the modeling and control of complex systems.

This includes *thin-film deposition* systems (e.g. pulsed laser deposition), a family of material growth techniques that work by iteratively depositing high-energy plasma, atomic, or molecular plumes on top of one another until they reach a desired thickness. As part of the [FastML for RHEED](https://github.com/abdelabd/CoaxlinkOcto_1cam_2404) project, I've led the design and development of an FPGA-accelerated ML algorithm for real-time, in-situ characterization of materials as they are being grown via thin-film deposition. Our design is very close to deployment, and it will enable us to study and control the highly energetic and non-equilibrium dynamics of thin-film deposition. 

On the high-energy physics side of things, I was recently award the [WATCHEP](https://watchep.org) Fellowship, a 2-year PhD fellowship and training program to prepare researchers in computational high-energy physics techniques. For this fellowship, I'm helping to expand the [Parnassus](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.19981) software, which uses large generative models to simulate particle detectors (e.g. ATLAS and CMS) in response to incoming particles during high-energy collision events - accelerating a crucial but extremely expensive step of any high-energy physics analysis. I'm developing the interface between this software and upstream simulators (e.g. [Pythia](https://pythia.org/)), as well as a surrogate model to accelerate the simulation of high-pileup events - typically done via Monte Carlo generators. Afterwards, I plan to use Parnassus to build an unbinned phase-space unfolding algorithm to estimate truth-level (or "generator-level") particle data based on reconstruction-level detector data. 

I also design FPGA algorithms for the [ATLAS](https://atlas.cern/) detector at CERN, mainly for the low-latency readout of data from the Pixel subdetector, which is responsible for charged-particle tracking. In the past, I published a [feasibility study](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/big-data/articles/10.3389/fdata.2022.828666/full) on the use of graph neural networks on FPGAs for charged-particle tracking at ATLAS and CMS.
